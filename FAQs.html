FAQs

What is Health Care?

add_circle_outline remove_circle_outline

Health care refers to medical care that is provided to improve and maintain the health of a person. It is done by diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of diseases or illness or injury.
Health care is provided by professionals in various fields such as physicians, doctors, nurses, pharmacist, dentist, midwives, etc.

What are the different types of Health Care?

add_circle_outline remove_circle_outline

Depending upon the types of care and services required health care is divided into three types:
i. Primary Health Care: The first contact for health care for most people is usually the primary health care generalist providing primary care. The people at this level are generalists. They are not specialists. Increasing number of patients with lifestyle diseases call for more primary health care facilities.
ii. Secondary Health Care: The health care provided by a specialist is known as secondary health care. This is the second tier in the system.
iii. Tertiary Health Care: Tertiary health care includes treatment that is advanced and complex. Tertiary health care includes services provided for major surgeries such as plastic surgery, cardiac surgery, and other complex surgeries. It also includes services provided for cancer management, treating severe burns, etc.


What are the different types of health care services?

add_circle_outline remove_circle_outline

Health care services are provided by specialist and health care providers are majorly divided into following categories:

i. Preventive care services: The steps taken to prevent a particular disease are known as preventive care. Here you are treating neither the disease nor the symptoms. These services include various immunization programs that help to prevent diseases.

ii. Promotion based Services: Generally provided by the government, these services create awareness and enable people to increase control of their health. In these services, health care education is provided to masses, people are thought about the basic hygiene which enables to prevent certain diseases etc. Anti-smoking campaigns, education about HIV/Aids, etc, all fall under this category.

iii. Curative Care Services: Here the main focus is to cure the disease and not to provide temporary relief from pain or to reduce stress.

iv. Rehabilitation Services: Rehabilitation services help people with disabilities to become self-reliant. These services fulfill the emotional, psychological and physical needs of the patients and their families.
